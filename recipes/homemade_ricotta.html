Homemade Ricotta

Recommended for fresh cheese lover

Fresh, light and creamy ricotta that will last for a few days.

ingredients

  • 1/2 gallon of whole milk (not pasteurized!) (you can replace 1-2 cups of full-fat milk with cream)
  • Salt
  • Lemon Juice / White vinegar

Accessories

  • Heavy bottom sauce pan with a lid
  • Wooden spoon
  • Slotted ladle
  • Bowl
  • Strainer
  • Cheesecloth
  • Ricotta basket
  • Thermometer (not mandatory)

instructions

  1. Place lightly dampened folded (3-4 times) cheese cloth on a strainer over a Bowl.
  2. Combine milk and salt in large sauce pan over medium heat until 85Â°C. Occasionally stir with wooden spoon.
  3. Reduce to low heat and add source of acidity - lemon / white vinegar. As the milk curdles, gently stir the mixture for around 2 minutes.
  4. Remove ricotta and place on the cheese cloth with the strainer over the bowl. (the liquid is whey)

    5. Reduce the draining time for a creamier consistency.
